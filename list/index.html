Friendly and delicious ice cream and candy shop

Mount Tom State Reservation

125 Reservation Road, Holyoke, MA. website
Hikes with beautiful views
There are several great hikes at Mount Tom. There is an ADA accessible trail near Lake Bray, and if you want to extend it a little you can go around the lake. The Goat Peak and Beau Bridges loop brings you first to Goat Peak with scenic views over the valley, and then down a path along a small stream. For a longer hike, the NET/M-M trail leads along the mountain ridge with great views at several spots.

Bridge of Flowers

22 Water St, Shelburne Falls, MA. website
A footbridge over the Deerfield River planted with flowers
This is a beautiful site for a short walk. There are also several nice shops, cafes, and restaurants in Shelburne Falls.

Whiting Street Reservoir

2 Mountain Park Rd, Holyoke, MA. website
A well-traveled path around Whiting Reservoir
The most convenient access to the Whiting Reservoir loop is at the end of Mountain Park Rd in Holyoke. After a short walk near the highway, there is a very nice loop around the Reservoir. Throughout, the wide gravel path is either flat or gently sloping. The full loop is just under 4 miles, and has beautiful views of the water and Mount Tom.

Mount Holyoke

10 Skinner State Park Road, Hadley, MA. website
A walk-up or drive-up mountain with spectacular views.
The top of Mount Holyoke has some of the best views in the area over the Connecticut River and the surrounding lands. The summit features an old hotel with a wrap-around porch as well as a number of picnic sites. You can either drive to the top, or walk up either on the roads or on paths through the woods.

Chesterfield Gorge

10 River Rd, Chesterfield, MA. website
Walk along a beautiful river
The Chesterfield Gorge is one of our favorite spots in Western Massachusetts. There is a small fee to park at the Trustees of Reservations site, which has views over the Gorge into the river below. From there, you can walk along a dirt road along the river side. There are several spots along the way where you can wade in the water. Walk as far as you want and turn around.

Childs Park

1 North Elm Street, Northampton, MA. website
A small city park with lovely gardens
Childs park is a great spot for a short walk around the park, a picnic, or a frisbee. It has paved paths with flower gardens, ponds, and wide lawns.

Fitzgerald Lake

101 North Farms Road, Northampton, MA. website
Trails through the woods near Fitzgerald Lake
The Fitzgerald Lake Conservation Area has a large network of trails alongside Fitzgerald Lake and through the adjacent views. There are four different entrances; from the entrance on North Farms Road, there is an accessible trail leading to the water. Beware -- it can be muddy and at times there are lots of mosquitos.

Mount Sugarloaf

300 Sugarloaf St, South Deerfield, MA. website
Mountaintop views of the Connecticut River and surrounding areas
The summit of Mount Sugarloaf offers great views of the valley below, with picnic sites. You can drive or hike to the top.

Amethyst Brook

4 Boltwood Avenue, Amherst, MA. website
Paths along a calm brook
The Amethyst Brook is a lovely small stream. There is a flat trail along the stream here, as well as access to the Robert Frost trail which leads up into adjacent hills.

Mount Toby

438 MA-63, Leverett, MA. website
Hikes with waterfalls and mountain-top views
Mount Toby has a fire tower at the summit with views of surrounding lands. The park also has a beautiful waterfall.

Look Park

300 North Main Street, Northampton, MA. website
A park with several fun attractions
Look park is a good place to walk around with accessible trails, a small zoo with native wildlife including native hawks and owls, a small train for children to ride around the park, mini golf, a water park, and pedal boats. There is a per-car admission fee and some of the activities cost extra, but overall it is a low cost and fun place to visit.

Paradise Pond and Mill River Trail

20 College Lane, Northampton, MA. website
A nice trail along the river behind Smith College
This trail starts across from the Botanic Gardens at Smith College and follows the Mill River. There is a short hill at the beginning, but it's otherwise flat. The best place for parking is either on Green St. or Elm St.

Smith College Botanic Gardens

16 College Lane, Northampton, MA. website
Indoor and outdoor gardens
The Smith college gardens include several large greenhouses with plants from a variety of climates including deserts, jungles, and so on. It's an especially inviting place to visit in winter. During the spring, summer, and fall, there are also beautiful gardens just outside the greenhouses that are also worth a visit.

Mittineague Park

Mittineague Park Rd and Park Dr, West Springfield, MA. website
A walk through forests and along the Westfield River
Mittineague Park has a 3-mile loop walk with views of the Westfield River and small streams nearby. There is a parking lot at the intersection of Mittineague Park Rd and Park Dr in West Springfield. From there, to get to the nature trail enter the park and turn left on Cowles Lane.

Bear Hole Reservoir

325 Great Plains Road, West Springfield, MA. website
Hiking trails around a reservoir and waterfalls
There are many trails near Bear Hole reservoir, including a roughly two-mile loop around the reservoir that passes by a waterfall.

Keystone Arch Bridges

1 Herbert Cross Rd, Chester, MA. website
A riverside walk with views of railroad bridges over the water
This is a beautiful walk along the river with views of waterfalls and old stone railroad bridges. The full walk is 5 miles, 2.5 miles out and back along the same route.

Chapel Falls and Ledges

1 Chapel Falls Rd, Conway, MA. website
A trail leading to a series of waterfalls and hilltop views
One one side of the road, there is a series of waterfalls with pools that you can swim in. On the other side, a trail leads up a hill with nice views at the top. Both of those trails have moderately steep sections. There are also trails leading into DAR State Park to the west and Bullitt Reservation to the north.
